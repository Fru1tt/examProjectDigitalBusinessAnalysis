# Project Proposal

## Project Title

Predicting Customer Channel Preference for Better Resource Allocation

## Group Members

- Bertine Josefsen
- Carl Henrik Broch Grude

## Problem Background

Retailers must decide how to allocate investments across online channels, physical stores, and omni-channel solutions. Customer behavior has shifted, and channel preference differs by customer segment. Poor understanding of these preferences can lead to inefficient marketing spend and suboptimal channel investments.

## Core Business Problem

A retailer cannot reliably predict whether a customer prefers:
- Online shopping
- In-store shopping
- Hybrid/omni-channel shopping

This uncertainty reduces decision quality for resource allocation and campaign targeting.

## Dataset

- Name: Online vs In-Store Shopping Behaviour Dataset
- Source: Kaggle
- Observations: 11,789
- Features: proposal scope mentions 26 features; current CSV file in this repo contains 25 columns in total.
- Includes demographics, digital habits, shopping behavior, trust/technology indicators, and channel preference outcome.

## Planned Analysis

1. Explore patterns and customer segments.
2. Identify key drivers of channel preference.
3. Build a prediction model for channel preference.
4. Present findings in a dashboard-style output for decision-makers.

## Expected Deliverable

A decision support solution that:
- Takes customer attributes as input.
- Predicts likely shopping preference.
- Explains key drivers behind predictions.

## Success Criteria

- Identify high-impact factors behind preference.
- Build a working predictive model with acceptable performance.
- Present clear outputs useful for managerial decisions.

## Known Limitation

The dataset is simulated and not collected from one specific retailer. Results should therefore be interpreted as a structured demonstration of data-driven business analysis rather than direct operational truth for a specific firm.
